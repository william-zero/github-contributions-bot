# The Secret Social Lives of Trees

Trees communicate with each other through underground fungal networks called mycorrhizae — often called the "Wood Wide Web." Through these networks, older "mother trees" can send sugars and nutrients to younger seedlings, even of different species.

Scientists have also discovered that trees release chemical signals through the air when under attack by insects, essentially "warning" their neighbors to boost their own defenses. A forest is less a collection of individuals and more a single, slow-thinking organism.
