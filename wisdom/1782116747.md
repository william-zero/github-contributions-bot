# The Maillard Reaction

The brown crust on toast, the sear on a steak, the golden color of cookies — all are the result of the Maillard reaction, a chemical dance between amino acids and reducing sugars that occurs above 140°C (280°F).

It produces hundreds of different flavor compounds simultaneously, which is why "browned" food tastes so much more complex than raw food. Technically, it's not caramelization (that's sugar-only chemistry) — but your tongue doesn't care about the distinction.
