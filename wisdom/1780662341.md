# The Streisand Effect

## The phenomenon where trying to hide something makes it more visible

In 2003, Barbara Streisand sued to have an aerial photo of her Malibu home removed from a coastal erosion study. Before the lawsuit, the photo had been viewed exactly 6 times. After the news broke, over 420,000 people visited the site in a month.

The lesson: attempting to suppress information online often backfires catastrophically, drawing exponentially more attention than if nothing had been done. The internet has a peculiar immune response to censorship — it treats restriction as a bug and routes around it, usually with great enthusiasm.

Modern examples crop up constantly: DMCA takedowns that go viral, "unpublished" company memos that trend on Twitter, and deleted tweets that get screenshotted into permanence. The digital age has made suppression not just difficult, but counterproductive.

**The takeaway:** Sometimes the best PR strategy is to let sleeping dogs lie. The cure can be far worse than the disease.
