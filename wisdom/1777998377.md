# The Ship of Theseus Problem

If you replace every plank of a ship one by one, is it still the same ship?

Philosophers have argued about this for 2,000 years. The twist: what if you collected all the old planks and rebuilt the *original* ship? Now you'd have two ships — which one is the *real* Ship of Theseus?

This isn't just ancient navel-gazing. Every cell in your body is replaced roughly every 7–10 years. Are you the same person who learned to ride a bike?

**Fun fact:** Humans replace about 330 billion cells per day — roughly 3.8 million per second. The "you" reading this sentence is already slightly different from the "you" who started it.
