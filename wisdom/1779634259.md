# Weekend Wisdom

**Did you know?** Otters hold hands while sleeping to prevent drifting apart. This behavior, called "rafting," is most commonly seen in sea otters who float on their backs in groups called rafts. By holding paws, they stay together as a family unit even as ocean currents try to pull them in different directions.

More fascinating: otters have the densest fur of any mammal — up to one million hairs per square inch — which keeps them warm without a thick layer of blubber like other marine mammals.

*Weekend thought: sometimes staying close to the ones you care about requires a little intentional effort.*
