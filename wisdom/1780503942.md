# The Dunning-Kruger Effect and the Mountain of Incompetence

The Dunning-Kruger Effect describes a cognitive bias where people with limited knowledge in a domain overestimate their competence, while genuine experts often underestimate theirs.

Psychologists David Dunning and Justin Kruger confirmed this in 1999: beginners who scored in the bottom quartile on logic tests estimated they'd performed near the top. Meanwhile, experts who aced the tests assumed most others had done equally well.

The cruel irony: to *know* what you don't know, you need enough expertise to recognize the gaps. Ignorance is invisible from inside itself. The cure for overconfidence isn't more confidence — it's more knowledge.
