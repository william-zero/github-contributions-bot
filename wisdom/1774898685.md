# Weekend Fact: Mantis Shrimp Vision

Mantis shrimp have 16 types of photoreceptors in their eyes — humans only have 3.
Despite this, they're actually worse at distinguishing between similar colors than we are.
They process colors differently, likely using each receptor as a simple threshold detector rather than doing comparative analysis like our brains do.
