# Dunning-Kruger: Why Incompetence Feels Like Expertise

The Dunning-Kruger effect is a cognitive bias where people with limited knowledge overestimate their competence — and experts underestimate theirs.

- Discovered in 1999 by psychologists David Dunning and Justin Kruger at Cornell
- Novices sit at the "Peak of Mt. Stupid" — just enough knowledge to feel confident, not enough to know what they don't know
- Experts often suffer from "the curse of knowledge" — they've forgotten how hard the basics once seemed
- The sweet spot is the "Plateau of Sustainability" — earned confidence backed by genuine skill

**The irony:** You need knowledge to recognize your own ignorance. True wisdom begins with knowing you don't know.

> "The fool doth think he is wise, but the wise man knows himself to be a fool." — Shakespeare, *As You Like It*
