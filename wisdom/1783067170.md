# The Surprisingly Useful Paradox of Boring Meetings

The average person will spend roughly 31 hours per month in unproductive meetings — enough time to watch the entire Lord of the Rings trilogy (extended editions) twice.

Counterintuitively, the most boring meetings are often the *most* critical ones. Studies show that emotionally flat, low-stakes check-ins are where actual decisions get quietly made, while exciting high-energy brainstorms mostly just produce whiteboards no one photographs.

**Bonus fact:** The Abilene Paradox describes a group of people collectively deciding to do something that none of them individually want to do, because everyone assumes everyone else wants to do it. Most meetings are the Abilene Paradox with coffee.
