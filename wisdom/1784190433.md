# Surprising Facts About Octopuses

- Octopuses have three hearts: two pump blood through the gills, and one pumps it through the body. When they swim, the heart that delivers blood to the body stops beating — which is why they prefer crawling.
- An octopus has no skeleton, allowing it to squeeze through any opening larger than its beak (the only hard part of its body).
- Octopus arms have a degree of autonomous intelligence; each arm can taste, touch, and make basic decisions independently without consulting the central brain.
- When threatened, an octopus can detach its own arm (autotomy) and regrow it later — the detached arm will continue moving to distract predators.
