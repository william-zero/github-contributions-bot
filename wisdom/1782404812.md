# Ship of Theseus and Continuous Identity

## The Classic Paradox

If you replace every plank in a ship one by one, is it still the same ship at the end?
Philosophers have argued about this for 2,000 years. So far: no consensus.
Heraclitus, who started it, is unavailable for comment.

## You Are Also a Ship

Every atom in your body is replaced roughly every 7 years.
The carbon atoms in your left thumb right now were probably inside a star before the Solar System existed.
You are a slow-motion Ship of Theseus made of stellar debris.

## The Coffee Twist

Your morning coffee atoms will become part of you briefly, then leave.
In this sense, your coffee does not fuel you — it *becomes* you, then escapes.
The cup, however, remains itself. The cup wins.

## Fun Number

Your body contains roughly 7 × 10²⁷ atoms.
If you replaced one per second, the full swap would take 2 × 10²⁰ years —
about 14 billion times the current age of the universe.
Good thing biology is parallelized.
