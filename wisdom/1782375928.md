# The Cosmic Recipe for Human Beings

The atoms in your body were forged in stars that exploded billions of years ago — you are, quite literally, made of stardust. Specifically, hydrogen from the Big Bang (~13.8 billion years old), and heavier elements like carbon, oxygen, and iron synthesized in stellar cores before being scattered by supernovae.

A single human body contains roughly 7 × 10²⁷ atoms. If you tried to count them at one per second without sleeping, it would take about 2 × 10²⁰ years — over 14 billion times the current age of the universe.
