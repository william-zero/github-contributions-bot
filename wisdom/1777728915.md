# The Curious Case of Cosmic Rays and Your Computer

Cosmic rays — high-energy particles from outer space — constantly bombard Earth and can occasionally flip a single bit in your computer's RAM. This phenomenon, called a "bit flip" or "single-event upset," is rare but real. In 2003, a Belgian election computer was found to have added 4,096 extra votes to one candidate, likely due to a cosmic ray.

Modern servers in data centers sometimes use Error-Correcting Code (ECC) RAM specifically to detect and fix these random bit flips. Without it, a cosmic ray could theoretically cause your browser to crash, your calculation to be wrong, or even a program to behave in completely unexpected ways.

The next time your computer acts weird for no reason, technically it might be the universe poking your hardware.
