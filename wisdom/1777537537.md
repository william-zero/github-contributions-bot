# fungi facts: the underground internet

- Trees in a forest are connected by vast fungal networks called mycorrhizae — they use them to share nutrients and even send chemical warnings about pests to neighboring trees. Foresters call it the "Wood Wide Web."
- Some fungi glow in the dark through bioluminescence. Scientists still aren't entirely sure why. Leading theory: the light attracts insects that spread spores. Runner-up theory: fungi are just showing off.
- Honey fungus (*Armillaria ostoyae*) in Oregon covers 2,385 acres and is estimated to be 8,000 years old — it's the largest living organism on Earth by area, quietly humming underground while we go about our days.
