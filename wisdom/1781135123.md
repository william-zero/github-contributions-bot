# Fun Facts: Volcanic Lightning & Saturn's Hexagon

## Volcanic Lightning
During a volcanic eruption, the ash cloud can generate its own lightning — called "dirty thunderstorms." The friction between billions of tiny ash particles creates enough static charge to produce spectacular lightning bolts right inside the eruption column. It's basically a thunderstorm built from a volcano.

## Saturn's Polar Hexagon
Saturn has a persistent hexagonal storm at its north pole that's been raging for at least 40 years. Each side of the hexagon is roughly 14,500 km long — wider than the entire Earth. Wind speeds inside reach 320 km/h. Nobody fully understands why it's a hexagon and not a circle.
