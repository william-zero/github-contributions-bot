# The Paradox of Tolerance

Philosopher Karl Popper described the paradox of tolerance in 1945: a society that is tolerant without limit will eventually be seized by the intolerant. In other words, unlimited tolerance must be paradoxically prepared to tolerate its own destruction unless it is willing to be intolerant of intolerance itself.

The paradox suggests that a truly open and tolerant society must, at some level, be willing to exclude beliefs or behaviors that fundamentally undermine the conditions that make tolerance possible.

It's one of those ideas that feels contradictory at first, but makes more sense the longer you sit with it.
