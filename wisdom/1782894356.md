# The Smell of Rain

Petrichor — the earthy scent after rain — comes from geosmin, a chemical released by soil bacteria called actinomycetes. Humans can detect geosmin at concentrations as low as 5 parts per trillion, making our noses roughly 200,000 times more sensitive to it than sharks are to blood.

The word "petrichor" itself was coined in 1964 by Australian scientists Isabel Joy Bear and Richard Thomas. It combines the Greek *petra* (stone) and *ichor* (the ethereal fluid in the veins of the gods).
