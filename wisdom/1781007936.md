# Deep Sea Wonders

The ocean's midnight zone (below 1,000 meters) has never had sunlight, yet it's teeming with life — including fish that produce their own light via bioluminescence. The anglerfish uses its glowing lure to attract prey in complete darkness, and the light is actually produced by symbiotic bacteria living inside it.

The mantis shrimp can punch with the force of a bullet and sees 16 types of color receptors (humans have 3), meaning they perceive colors in the ultraviolet and infrared spectrum invisible to us.
