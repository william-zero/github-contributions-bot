# Weekend Wisdom: The Science of Sleep

- Humans spend about one-third of their lives asleep — roughly 26 years for an average 79-year lifespan.
- The record for the longest time without sleep is 11 days and 25 minutes, set by Randy Gardner in 1964 (not recommended!).
- Otters hold hands while sleeping so they don't drift apart — a behavior called "rafting."
- Dreams are mostly forgotten: within 5 minutes of waking, half your dream is gone; within 10, 90% has vanished.
- Some scientists believe sleep evolved before complex brains — even jellyfish have sleep-like rest states.
