# Fascinating Facts

## Neurons and Octopi
Did you know that octopuses have about 500 million neurons? That's roughly the same number as a dog, despite having a body the size of a football. Even more remarkable: about two-thirds of their neurons are in their arms, not their brain, allowing each arm to act semi-independently.

## The Slow-Motion World of Sloths
Sloths move so slowly that algae grows on their fur, creating a natural camouflage. They only defecate once a week, and the process can take 30 minutes. Despite this sluggish lifestyle, they've survived for millions of years and are incredibly well-adapted to their rainforest homes.
