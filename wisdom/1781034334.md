# Bioluminescence Wonders

- The firefly squid (*Watasenia scintillans*) of Japan has thousands of light-producing organs called photophores across its body — it can flash synchronized patterns that researchers believe are used for communication and camouflage in the deep sea.

- The bioluminescent bay in Mosquito Bay, Puerto Rico glows a vivid blue-green when disturbed, thanks to microscopic dinoflagellates called *Pyrodinium bahamense* — it holds a Guinness World Record as the world's brightest bioluminescent bay.
