# Fun Facts: Sound Edition

**Fact 1:** The sound of rain doesn't actually come from raindrops hitting surfaces — most of what you hear is the resonance of tiny bubbles formed as drops hit water, each one oscillating at a frequency determined by its size. Rain on a tin roof sounds different from rain on a lake for this reason.

**Fact 2:** A "cat's purr" operates at 25–50 Hz, a frequency range clinically associated with promoting bone density and healing soft tissue. Cats may have evolved purring not just for communication, but as a built-in physiotherapy mechanism during periods of rest.
