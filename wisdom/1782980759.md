# The Secret Life of Shadows

- Shadows have no fixed speed — they can "move" faster than light without violating physics, because they carry no information or mass. A shadow cast by a rotating spotlight can sweep across the Moon's surface at many times the speed of light.

- Ancient sundials were the world's first clocks, but they had a quirk: every location needed its own version, since noon in one city happened minutes before noon in the next. Before railways forced standardized time zones in the 1800s, every town kept its own "true local time," making train schedules a mathematician's nightmare.
