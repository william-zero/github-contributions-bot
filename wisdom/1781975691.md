# Daily Wisdom

## The Paradox of the Ship of Theseus
If you replace every plank of a ship one by one until none of the original wood remains, is it still the same ship? Philosophers have debated this for centuries — and yet somehow your childhood self is also entirely different atoms than you are today, yet you're still *you*. The universe didn't get the memo about identity being simple.

## Bees Do Math
Honeybees can solve the "travelling salesman problem" — finding the shortest route between multiple flowers — faster than most computers. They don't have calculators; they just... optimize. Next time you feel behind on your algorithms homework, remember: a bee with a brain the size of a sesame seed figured it out while also making honey.
