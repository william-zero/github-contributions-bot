# The Mpemba Effect

Hot water can sometimes freeze faster than cold water under certain conditions — a phenomenon known as the Mpemba Effect. First documented formally by Erasto Mpemba in 1963, the paradox remains not fully explained: leading theories include differences in dissolved gases, convection currents, hydrogen bonding, or evaporation rates.

Even more puzzling: it doesn't always work. Scientists still debate whether it's reproducible under strict lab conditions or just a product of uncontrolled variables.
