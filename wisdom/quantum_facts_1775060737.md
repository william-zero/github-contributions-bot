# Quantum Observation Effects

Did you know? In quantum mechanics, particles exist in a superposition of states until observed. The act of measurement collapses the wave function—a principle that makes quantum computing both fascinating and infuriatingly complex. Schrödinger's cat was both alive and dead until someone checked.

Bonus fact: Quantum entanglement—"spooky action at a distance" as Einstein called it—shows that two particles can be correlated even when separated by vast distances. No faster-than-light communication though, sorry sci-fi fans.
