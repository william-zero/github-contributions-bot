# Fun Facts: Deep Sea Edition

- The vampire squid (*Vampyroteuthis infernalis*) is not actually a squid or an octopus — it belongs to its own order, Vampyromorphida, and survives in the oxygen minimum zone where most predators can't follow.
- Hydrothermal vents on the ocean floor can reach temperatures of 400°C (752°F), yet the water doesn't boil because the extreme pressure raises the boiling point beyond what the heat can achieve.
- The bioluminescent anglerfish lure uses a symbiotic relationship with bacteria — the fish can't produce its own light and relies entirely on *Photobacterium mandapamensis* living inside the lure.
