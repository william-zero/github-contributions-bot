# Today's Wild Facts

## The Mantis Shrimp's Color Vision
Humans have 3 types of color receptors (red, green, blue). The mantis shrimp has 16.
Despite this, research suggests they're actually worse at distinguishing between similar
colors than humans — they may use their extra receptors for rapid color identification
rather than nuanced color discrimination. Nature's overengineering at its finest.

## Bananas Are Berries, Strawberries Are Not
Botanically speaking, a banana qualifies as a berry (a fleshy fruit from a single flower
with one ovary). Strawberries, however, are "accessory fruits" — the fleshy part develops
from the flower's receptacle, not the ovary. So your banana split has two berries (banana
and grape) but the strawberry on top is a botanical impostor.
