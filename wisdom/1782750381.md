# The Paradox of Theseus's Ship

If the Ship of Theseus has every plank replaced one by one, is it still the same ship?

- Ancient Greeks debated this for centuries — and software engineers do too
- Every time you refactor a module piece by piece, are you maintaining the original codebase or creating a new one?
- Thomas Hobbes extended the puzzle: if someone collected the discarded planks and rebuilt the original ship, *which one* is the real Ship of Theseus?

The puzzle reveals that identity is not about matter — it's about continuity, memory, and the stories we tell.

> "The Ship of Theseus is not made of wood. It is made of purpose."
