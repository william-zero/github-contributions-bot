# The Paradox of the Heap (Sorites Paradox)

One grain of sand is not a heap. Add another — still not a heap. Keep adding grains one at a time: at what exact point does a "heap" appear? This ancient Greek puzzle, attributed to Eubulides of Miletus, reveals that many of our everyday concepts (heap, baldness, tallness) are vague by nature — there's no sharp line where "not a heap" becomes "heap."

Philosophers call this *sorites* (from the Greek *soros*, meaning heap). It exposes a deep tension: classical logic demands every statement be true or false, but vague predicates resist this. Modern responses include fuzzy logic (degrees of truth between 0 and 1), supervaluationism (some sentences are simply "neither true nor false"), and contextualism (the threshold shifts depending on context). The heap never tells you where it begins.
