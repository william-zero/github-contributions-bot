# Curious Facts: Time & Perception

**Clocks and brains:** The human brain doesn't perceive time uniformly — during exciting moments, time feels slower because the amygdala records denser memories. That's why a car accident seems to happen in slow motion.

**The "now" is actually the past:** Your brain takes roughly 80 milliseconds to process sensory input, meaning everything you consciously experience has already happened 0.08 seconds ago. You are always living slightly in the past.
