# The Paradox of the Heap

**Sorites Paradox**: If you have a heap of sand and remove one grain, you still have a heap. Remove another — still a heap. Keep going grain by grain. At what exact grain does the heap stop being a heap?

This ancient Greek puzzle (attributed to Eubulides of Miletus, ~4th century BC) exposes a fundamental flaw in reasoning about vague predicates. There is no clean threshold — language is fuzzy, and the real world doesn't care about our categories.

**Bonus fact**: The word "sorites" comes from the Greek *soros* (σωρός), meaning heap or pile. Philosophers have spent 2,400 years arguing about sand.
