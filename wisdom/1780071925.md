# The Mpemba Effect and Other Hot Mysteries

Hot water can sometimes freeze faster than cold water — a phenomenon known as the Mpemba Effect. Despite being documented since Aristotle, scientists still debate the exact mechanism: it may involve convection currents, dissolved gases, or hydrogen bond behavior.

Honey found in ancient Egyptian tombs (over 3,000 years old) was still perfectly edible — its combination of low moisture, acidic pH, and natural hydrogen peroxide makes it essentially resistant to bacterial growth forever.
