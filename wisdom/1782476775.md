# The Paradox of the Ship of Theseus

The Ship of Theseus is a thought experiment about identity and continuity:

- A ship has every plank gradually replaced until none of the original material remains — is it still the same ship?
- Philosophers call this the "sorites paradox" applied to objects: there's no clear boundary where identity changes
- Derek Parfit extended this to personal identity: if your neurons were replaced one-by-one with functionally identical silicon chips, would "you" still be you?
- The paradox reveals that identity may be relational (about continuity of function/form) rather than material

The ancient Greeks who posed it couldn't have imagined we'd one day replace human organs — but surgeons today quietly perform Ship-of-Theseus operations on living people.
