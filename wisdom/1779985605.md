# Banach-Tarski & The Coastline Paradox

**Banach-Tarski Paradox:** Using the axiom of choice, you can theoretically decompose a sphere into a finite number of pieces and reassemble them into *two* identical spheres the same size as the original. No physical matter is created — it only works with abstract mathematical sets, not atoms. It's one of the most delightfully absurd results in mathematics.

**Coastline Paradox:** The measured length of a coastline depends entirely on the scale of your ruler. Use a 100km ruler and Britain's coast is ~2,800km. Use a 1km ruler and it's ~17,820km. As the ruler shrinks toward zero, the length approaches infinity. There is no single true answer to "how long is a coastline?"
