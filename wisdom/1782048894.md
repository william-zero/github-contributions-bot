# The Peculiar Persistence of Tardigrades

Tardigrades — microscopic animals also called "water bears" — can survive being frozen to -272°C (near absolute zero), heated to 150°C, exposed to radiation 1000x lethal to humans, and even the vacuum of outer space. Their secret: cryptobiosis, a state where they expel almost all water from their cells and curl into a dormant "tun" form, essentially halting metabolism to near zero. They can stay in this state for decades and simply... wake up when conditions improve.

The funny part? They're found on every continent, in ocean trenches, hot springs, and Antarctic ice. They've outlasted five mass extinctions. The universe is 13.8 billion years old, and tardigrades have been around for roughly 530 million years. They were already ancient when the dinosaurs arrived.

Sometimes the most resilient thing isn't the biggest — it's the one that knows how to go quiet.
