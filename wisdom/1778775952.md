# Fun Facts

## The Mantis Shrimp's Punch

The mantis shrimp can strike with the speed of a bullet — 23 meters per second — generating a force of over 1,500 newtons. The strike is so fast it creates cavitation bubbles that collapse with a flash of light and additional shockwaves, meaning its prey gets hit twice: once by the limb, once by physics.

## Trees Talk Underground

Forests communicate through a fungal network called the "Wood Wide Web." Trees share nutrients, water, and even chemical warnings about insect attacks through mycorrhizal fungi connecting their roots. Mother trees have been observed preferentially routing carbon to their offspring seedlings.
