# Parkinson's Law — Work Expands to Fill the Time Available

Cyril Northcote Parkinson observed in 1955 that "work expands so as to fill the time available for its completion." A task that could be done in an hour will somehow take all day if that's how long you have.

This explains why tight deadlines often produce better focus than open-ended schedules — scarcity of time is oddly clarifying.
