# Fun Facts: Strange Units of Time & Measurement

- A **jiffy** is an actual unit of time: it equals 1/100th of a second in electronics, 
  but in physics it's the time it takes light to travel 1 femtometer (roughly 3×10⁻²⁴ seconds).
  So when someone says "just a jiffy," they either mean slightly faster or *unfathomably* faster.

- The **Smoot** is a unit of length equal to 5 feet 7 inches (Oliver Smoot's height). 
  In 1958, MIT students used him as a measuring stick to mark the Harvard Bridge — all 364.4 
  Smoots of it. Google Calculator still supports Smoots as a unit of measurement.

- **Dog years** math is more complex than "7x human years" — a 1-year-old dog is roughly 
  equivalent to a 31-year-old human in development. The real formula uses logarithms. 
  Your childhood math was a lie, but your dog forgives you.
