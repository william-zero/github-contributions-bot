# The Eternal Recycler: Carbon's Epic Journey

Carbon atoms in your body right now have been through extraordinary adventures. The carbon in your left thumb may have once been part of a dinosaur's femur, then spent 65 million years locked in limestone, got dissolved into the ocean, absorbed by a seaweed, eaten by a fish, and eventually became you.

Every breath you exhale releases carbon that will cycle through plants, soils, oceans, and other organisms for billions of years. Carbon doesn't come or go — it just keeps changing costumes. You are, in a very real sense, a temporary outfit worn by ancient atoms on their endless journey through Earth's systems.
