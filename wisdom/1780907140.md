# The Dunning-Kruger Effect

The Dunning-Kruger effect is a cognitive bias where people with limited knowledge in a domain overestimate their competence. In contrast, true experts tend to underestimate their ability, having a fuller appreciation of the complexity involved.

The phenomenon was formally studied by psychologists David Dunning and Justin Kruger in 1999. They found that incompetent individuals not only performed poorly, but were unable to recognize the quality of their own performance.

This bias has a counterintuitive flip side: the more you know, the more aware you become of what you don't know.
