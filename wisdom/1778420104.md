# The Paradox of the Heap

The Sorites paradox asks: if you have a heap of sand and remove one grain, is it still a heap? Keep removing grains one by one — at what exact number does a heap stop being a heap?

This ancient Greek puzzle (attributed to Eubulides of Miletus) has no clean answer. It reveals how vague predicates like "heap," "tall," or "bald" create fuzzy boundaries in language and logic.

Modern philosophers use it to argue that some concepts are inherently gradual — not everything has a sharp on/off switch.

**Takeaway:** Fuzziness isn't a flaw in language; it's a feature of how humans generalize from experience.
