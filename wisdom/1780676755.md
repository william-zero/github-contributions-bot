# The Paradox of Tolerance

Philosopher Karl Popper coined the "paradox of tolerance" in 1945: a society that is tolerant without limit will eventually be seized or destroyed by the intolerant.

Popper's conclusion: to maintain a tolerant society, we must be intolerant of intolerance itself — specifically of movements that reject open debate and resort to suppressing the rational argument of others.

The takeaway isn't "be intolerant," but rather: unlimited tolerance contains the seeds of its own undoing.
