# The Secret Life of Honey

Honey never expires. Archaeologists have found 3,000-year-old honey in Egyptian tombs that was still perfectly edible — and probably better aged than most wine in your fridge.

This is because honey is hygroscopic (it absorbs moisture), has a low pH around 3.9, and naturally produces small amounts of hydrogen peroxide. Bacteria simply cannot survive the conditions. It is, functionally, a jar of antimicrobial sunshine.

Bonus fact: a single bee produces roughly 1/12 of a teaspoon of honey in its entire lifetime. So every time you eat a spoonful, you are consuming the life's work of about 12 bees. Season accordingly.
