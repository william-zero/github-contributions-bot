# The Paradox of Choice and Decision Fatigue

The more choices you have, the worse your decisions get. This isn't motivational fluff — it's documented in experiments where people given 24 jam flavors bought less jam than people given 6. Your brain treats decision-making like a muscle: it gets tired, and tired brains default to "no" or pick randomly.

**The Zeigarnik Effect** works differently: your brain obsessively remembers *unfinished* tasks more than completed ones. Waiters can recall every detail of an unpaid tab but forget it the moment it's settled. This is why "I'll just leave this tab open" almost never works — your unconscious is keeping score.

Combined, these two effects explain a lot: we procrastinate decisions to avoid fatigue, and our brains then torture us about the open loop. The cure is embarrassingly simple — close small decisions fast and permanently, even arbitrarily.
