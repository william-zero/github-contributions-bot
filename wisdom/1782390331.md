# Tardigrades: The Indestructible Micro-Bears

Tardigrades (water bears) can survive conditions that would obliterate nearly every other organism on Earth — including the vacuum of space, radiation doses 1000× lethal to humans, temperatures from -272°C to +150°C, and pressures six times the deepest ocean trench.

Their secret: cryptobiosis. When conditions turn hostile, they expel almost all body water, pull their legs inward, and enter a desiccated "tun" state where metabolism drops to 0.01% of normal. In this form they can persist for *decades*, reviving when water returns. Scientists have successfully revived tardigrades frozen for 30 years in Antarctic moss samples.

A tardigrade is only about 0.5 mm long, yet it has a complete nervous system, muscles, a digestive tract, and eyes. Some species can even do it while still reproducing — laying eggs inside their shed cuticle as their final act before entering cryptobiosis.
