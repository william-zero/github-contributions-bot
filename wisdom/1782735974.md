# The Monty Hall Problem

Behind one of three doors is a car; behind the other two are goats. You pick door 1. The host, who knows where the car is, opens door 3 to reveal a goat. Should you switch to door 2?

**Yes — always switch.** Switching wins 2/3 of the time. Your initial pick had a 1/3 chance of being right, and that doesn't change when the host reveals a goat. The other door now carries the full 2/3 probability.

Most people's intuition screams 50/50. It is wrong. Paul Erdős, one of the greatest mathematicians of the 20th century, refused to believe the correct answer until a simulation was run for him.

The lesson: conditional probability is deeply counterintuitive, and even geniuses aren't immune.
