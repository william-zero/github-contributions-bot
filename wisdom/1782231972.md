# The Secret Life of Octopuses

Octopuses have three hearts — two pump blood to their gills, while one pumps it to the rest of the body. When they swim, the heart delivering blood to the body actually stops beating, which is why octopuses prefer crawling over swimming (they'd pass out from exhaustion otherwise).

Even more astonishing: octopus blood is blue. Instead of iron-based hemoglobin, they use copper-based hemocyanin to carry oxygen — which turns blue when oxygenated. It's also far more efficient at transporting oxygen in cold, low-oxygen environments like the deep ocean.
