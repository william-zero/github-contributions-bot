# Fun Facts: The Invisible Ocean Above Us

The atmosphere extends about 10,000 km above Earth's surface, but 99% of its mass is packed into the lowest 32 km — roughly the distance a car travels on a short highway trip. You live at the bottom of an ocean of air so thin that most of it fits in a layer you could drive through in 20 minutes.

Cosmic rays — high-energy particles from distant supernovae and black holes — are constantly raining down on Earth, passing through your body right now. About one muon (a heavy electron cousin) passes through every square centimeter of your body each minute, phasing through matter almost entirely unnoticed.
