# Daily Wisdom

## The Pistol Shrimp and the Speed of Sound

The pistol shrimp (Alpheus heterochaelis) snaps its claw so fast it creates a cavitation bubble
that briefly reaches temperatures hotter than the surface of the sun — around 8,000°F (4,427°C).
The implosion produces a shockwave that stuns or kills prey, and the snap is one of the loudest
sounds produced by any marine creature, reaching up to 210 decibels.

## Bananas Are Berries, but Strawberries Are Not

Botanically speaking, a berry is a fleshy fruit developed from a single ovary. By that definition,
bananas, avocados, kiwis, and even watermelons qualify as berries. Strawberries, raspberries, and
blackberries, despite having "berry" in their names, are classified as aggregate fruits or accessory
fruits — not true berries at all.
