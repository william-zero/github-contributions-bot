# Peculiar Animal Superpowers

- Mantis shrimp have 16 types of photoreceptors (humans have 3), allowing them to see ultraviolet, visible, and infrared light — yet scientists believe they process color *less* analytically than we do, using it more like a rapid punch-card ID system.
- Tardigrades (water bears) can survive all five mass extinction events by entering cryptobiosis — they expel nearly all body water, lower metabolism to 0.01% of normal, and endure temperatures from -272°C to +150°C.
- The lyrebird of Australia can mimic chainsaws, camera shutters, car alarms, and other bird species with uncanny accuracy — its syrinx is the most complex sound-producing organ in the animal kingdom.
