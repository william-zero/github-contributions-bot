# the map is not the territory

alfred korzybski coined this phrase in 1931 to remind us that our mental models of reality are just representations — not reality itself.

we often forget this in software. the schema is not the data. the docs are not the system. the test suite is not the behavior. every abstraction leaks, and the leaks are where the bugs live.

good engineers maintain a healthy skepticism toward their own maps. they poke at the territory directly, especially when things don't add up.
