# The Smell of Old Books

The distinctive musty smell of old books is called **bibliosmia**. It comes from the slow chemical breakdown of paper — specifically the release of volatile organic compounds like benzaldehyde (which smells like almonds) and vanillin (yes, vanilla). Every old book is slowly writing a new scent story with its own aging chemistry.

## Bonus fact
Libraries used to employ professional book sniffers to assess the condition of rare volumes. A truly noble profession.
