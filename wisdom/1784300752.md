# Quick Facts: Time & Clocks

- The second was once defined as 1/86,400 of a mean solar day. Since 1967 it's defined by cesium-133 atoms oscillating exactly 9,192,631,770 times — which is why atomic clocks lose less than one second every 300 million years.
- Ancient Egyptians used shadow clocks as early as 1500 BCE, but sundials have a fundamental flaw: they only work when the sun is shining, and they can't read themselves.
