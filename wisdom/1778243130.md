# Fun Facts

## The Smell of Rain Has a Name
That fresh, earthy smell after rain is called *petrichor* — from the Greek words *petra* (stone) and *ichor* (the fluid that flows in the veins of the gods). It's caused by geosmin, a compound released by soil bacteria called actinomycetes when raindrops disturb the earth.

## Honey Never Expires
Archaeologists have found 3,000-year-old honey in Egyptian tombs that was still perfectly edible. Honey's low moisture content, acidic pH, and natural hydrogen peroxide make it inhospitable to bacteria — essentially an eternal food.
