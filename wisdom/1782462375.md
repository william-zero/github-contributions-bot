# The Mpemba Effect

Hot water can sometimes freeze faster than cold water — a counterintuitive phenomenon known as the Mpemba Effect, named after Tanzanian student Erasto Mpemba who noticed it while making ice cream in 1963.

The exact mechanism is still debated among physicists, but proposed explanations include faster evaporation reducing the mass of hot water, differences in dissolved gas content, and convection currents creating more uniform cooling. Nature loves to keep us humble.
