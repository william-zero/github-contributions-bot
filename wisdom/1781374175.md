# Weekend Curiosities

## The Paradox of Freshly Mowed Grass
That smell you love after cutting grass? It's actually a chemical distress signal — plants releasing green leaf volatiles to warn neighbors of damage. You're basically inhaling a lawn screaming for help. And yet, it's deeply calming to humans. Nature is bizarre.

## Saturday Is Named After Saturn
Saturday is the only day of the English week still directly named after a Roman god (Saturn). Sunday and Monday nod to the Sun and Moon, but all the others — Tuesday through Friday — were long ago overwritten by Norse gods. Saturn held his ground.

## Clouds Are Surprisingly Heavy
A typical fluffy cumulus cloud weighs around 500,000 kg — roughly the same as 80 elephants. It stays aloft purely because the water droplets making it up are so tiny that air resistance keeps them suspended. The sky is full of invisible freight.
