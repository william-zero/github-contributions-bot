# Random Facts: Octopus & Lightning Edition

- An octopus has three hearts: two pump blood to its gills, and one pumps it to the rest of the body. When an octopus swims, the heart that delivers blood to its body stops beating, which is why they prefer crawling — swimming exhausts them.
- A single bolt of lightning is about 1 inch wide but can reach temperatures of 30,000 Kelvin — roughly five times hotter than the surface of the sun. The thunder you hear is the sound of air rapidly expanding from that intense heat.
