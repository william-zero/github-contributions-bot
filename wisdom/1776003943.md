# Weekend Wonder: The Mantis Shrimp

The mantis shrimp has 16 types of color receptors — humans only have 3.
Despite this, studies suggest they may actually process color *worse*
than us, using the extra receptors as a kind of rapid barcode scanner
rather than mixing signals together.

So more isn't always better. Sometimes the brain's interpretation
matters more than the raw input.
