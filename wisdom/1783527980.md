# The Science of Déjà Vu

Déjà vu is that eerie sensation that you've experienced something before when you haven't. Neuroscientists believe it occurs when the brain's memory-checking system fires slightly out of sync — creating a false sense of familiarity.

Studies show déjà vu happens most often to people aged 15–25, during periods of stress or fatigue, and to frequent travelers. It may actually be a sign your brain's error-detection system is working correctly, catching a misfired memory match.

Interestingly, people with epilepsy sometimes experience prolonged déjà vu as a type of seizure aura — providing researchers a window into how memory recognition actually works in the brain.
