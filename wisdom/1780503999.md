# The Baader-Meinhof Phenomenon (Frequency Illusion)

Ever learn a new word and then suddenly hear it everywhere? Buy a red car and notice red cars on every street? That's the Baader-Meinhof Phenomenon — also called the frequency illusion.

Two cognitive processes collaborate on the trick: first, *selective attention* makes your brain flag the newly learned thing as notable. Second, *confirmation bias* leads you to remember every subsequent occurrence while ignoring the many times you didn't see it.

The thing hasn't gotten more common. Your brain just started keeping score.
