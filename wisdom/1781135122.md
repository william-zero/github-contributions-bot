# Fun Facts: Lightning & Sand

- A single lightning bolt is only about 1-2 inches (2-5 cm) wide, but it can be 5 miles (8 km) long and reaches temperatures of around 30,000 Kelvin — about 5 times hotter than the surface of the sun.
- When lightning strikes sand, it can fuse the grains together into a glassy, branching structure called a "fulgurite" — essentially a fossilized lightning bolt preserved in glass.
