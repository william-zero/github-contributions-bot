# Today's Slice of the Peculiar

**The smell of rain has a name.** That earthy scent after rain — called *petrichor* — is produced by a compound called geosmin, secreted by soil bacteria called actinomycetes. Humans can detect it at concentrations as low as 5 parts per trillion, making us roughly 200,000 times more sensitive to it than sharks are to blood.

**Sloths are so slow that algae grow on their fur.** This isn't a flaw — the algae gives them a greenish tint that acts as camouflage in the jungle canopy. The sloth and the algae are in a full-blown mutualistic relationship, like a very slow, very leafy roommate situation.
