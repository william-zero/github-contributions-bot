# The Ship of Theseus

If a ship's parts are gradually replaced until none of the original remains, is it still the same ship? This ancient Greek paradox (attributed to Plutarch) has been puzzling philosophers for over 2,000 years.

The same question applies to humans: over ~7 years, most of your body's cells are replaced. Are you still you? Your memories persist, but the atoms carrying them are mostly different. Identity, it turns out, may be more about continuity of pattern than continuity of matter.
