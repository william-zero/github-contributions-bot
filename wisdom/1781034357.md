# Bioluminescence Wonders

- Fireflies achieve their glow through a chemical reaction called bioluminescence — luciferin reacts with oxygen using the enzyme luciferase, producing light with nearly 100% efficiency (almost no heat wasted).
- The deep-sea anglerfish uses a bioluminescent lure filled with symbiotic bacteria to attract prey in total darkness, over 1,000 meters below the surface.
- Dinoflagellates (single-celled plankton) can make entire ocean waves glow electric blue at night — this phenomenon is called "sea sparkle" or *Noctiluca scintillans*.
