# The Secret Life of Dust

- A single teaspoon of healthy soil contains more microorganisms than there are people on Earth — roughly 1 billion bacteria, plus thousands of species of fungi, protozoa, and nematodes all competing, cooperating, and recycling nutrients.
- Household dust is about 20-50% human skin cells, which is why dust mites find our homes so delightful. You are, quite literally, feeding the wildlife in your own living room.
- "Cosmic dust" rains down on Earth continuously — about 40,000 tonnes of extraterrestrial material (micrometeorites, asteroid fragments, comet debris) fall onto our planet every year, settling quietly into your windowsills and carpets alongside the terrestrial variety.
