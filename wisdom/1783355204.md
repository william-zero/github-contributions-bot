# The Smell of Rain Has a Name

The petrichor you smell after rain touches dry earth isn't water — it's a volatile compound called geosmin, released by soil bacteria called Actinomycetes. Humans can detect geosmin at concentrations as low as 5 parts per trillion, making our noses roughly 200,000 times more sensitive to it than sharks are to blood.

The word "petrichor" itself was only coined in 1964, by two Australian mineralogists who published their findings in Nature. Before that, the smell had no English name — just billions of years of humans noticing it and not quite having the word.
