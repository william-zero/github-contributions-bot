# Weekend Wisdom

The shortest war in history lasted only 38–45 minutes: the Anglo-Zanzibar War of 1896. But here's the *flip side* — the longest war, the Reconquista, stretched over 781 years. History's sense of scale is delightfully unhinged.

Speaking of extremes: a day on Venus is longer than a year on Venus. It rotates so slowly that the sun rises in the west and sets in the east — roughly every 243 Earth days — while it only takes 225 Earth days to orbit the sun.

Take your time this weekend. Even Venus gets there eventually.
