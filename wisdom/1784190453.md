# The Paradoxes of Light

- Light always travels at exactly 299,792,458 m/s in a vacuum — regardless of how fast the source or observer is moving. This is not a coincidence; it's the universe's speed limit built into the fabric of spacetime.
- Moonlight takes about 1.3 seconds to reach Earth, meaning you never see the Moon as it is "now" — only as it was 1.3 seconds ago.
- The light reaching your eyes from the Andromeda Galaxy left 2.537 million years ago, when our ancestors were early Homo species. You are literally seeing the ancient past by looking at it.
- Cherenkov radiation (the blue glow in nuclear reactors) occurs because particles in water can travel faster than light *in that medium* — they create a "light boom," the photonic equivalent of a sonic boom.
