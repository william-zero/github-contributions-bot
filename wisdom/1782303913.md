# The Mantis Shrimp's Incredible Vision

Mantis shrimp have 16 types of color receptors — humans have just 3. Yet studies suggest they
process color *differently*, not better: instead of mixing signals, each receptor fires
independently, giving them a lightning-fast parallel color-detection system. It's less like
seeing more colors and more like running 16 separate cameras simultaneously.

Their punch is equally wild: the dactyl clubs accelerate at 10,000g — the same force as a
.22 caliber bullet — and cavitation bubbles from the strike deliver a *second* shockwave even
after the physical blow lands. A 6-inch crustacean that hits twice.
