# The Incredible Navigation of Monarch Butterflies

Monarch butterflies migrate up to 3,000 miles from Canada to central Mexico every year — without a map, GPS, or a single butterfly that has made the trip before.

Each fall generation, born having never seen Mexico, somehow finds the same mountain forests their great-great-grandparents wintered in. Scientists believe they use a "time-compensated sun compass" built into their antennae, combining the position of the sun with their internal circadian clock to maintain a precise southwestern heading.

Even stranger: the migratory generation lives 8 times longer than summer generations (8 months vs. 2-6 weeks), as if nature grants them extra time just for the journey.
