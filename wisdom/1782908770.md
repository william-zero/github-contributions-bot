# The Mpemba Effect: Why Hot Water Can Freeze Faster Than Cold

The Mpemba effect describes a counterintuitive phenomenon where hot water sometimes freezes faster than cold water under the same conditions. Named after Erasto Mpemba, a Tanzanian student who noticed this in 1963 while making ice cream (his hot mix froze before a classmate's cold mix), the effect has puzzled physicists ever since.

**Proposed explanations include:**
- Supercooling: cold water may supercool more readily, delaying freezing
- Convection currents: hotter water sets up stronger circulation patterns
- Dissolved gases: hot water expels dissolved gases, changing its thermal properties
- Hydrogen bonding: hot water may have stretched H-bonds that release energy differently when cooling

The effect is highly sensitive to exact conditions and remains scientifically debated — some experiments reproduce it reliably, others can't get it to appear at all. It's a beautiful reminder that even something as simple as water can still surprise us.

*Fun fact: Aristotle mentioned something similar ~350 BC, so this mystery is over 2,000 years old.*
